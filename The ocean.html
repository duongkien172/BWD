Vietnam's sea: Beauty and Potential

 

Vietnam is blessed by nature with a coastline stretching over 3,200 km, running from Mong Cai (Quang Ninh) to Ha Tien (Kien Giang), embracing 28 coastal provinces and cities. It boasts beaches that captivate the hearts of visitors with their fine white sand, turquoise waters, and rows of poetic coconut palms, creating a pristine and majestic natural landscape that attracts tourists from all over the world.

  • Diverse Beaches: Vietnam's coastline boasts a diverse and rich system of beaches, catering to every tourist's needs. From the bustling and vibrant beaches of Nha Trang, Da Nang, and Phu Quoc, to the pristine and peaceful beaches of Con Dao, Ly Son, and Mui Ne, each location bears a unique charm that captivates visitors.

  • Rich Marine Ecosystem: Vietnam's coastline is home to a rich and diverse marine ecosystem with numerous rare and precious marine species. Here, you'll find vibrant coral reefs, schools of fish swimming freely, and countless other marine creatures, creating a fantastical and lively underwater world.
Vietnam's coral reefs

  • Unique Cultural Heritage: Vietnam's coastline also preserves a wealth of unique cultural heritage, deeply intertwined with the history and lives of local people. Traditional crafts like fish sauce making, fish drying, and net weaving, along with vibrant festivals like the Whale Festival (Nha Trang) and the Cau Ngu Festival (Quang Ninh), create the distinctive cultural character of Vietnam's coastal regions.

Protecting Vietnam's Sea:

Vietnam's coastline is a precious national resource that must be protected and preserved for future generations. Every Vietnamese citizen needs to raise awareness of marine environmental protection, avoid littering, and refrain from illegal fishing. Engage in marine conservation activities like planting trees along the coast, collecting coastal waste, and contributing to keeping Vietnam's coastline green, clean, and beautiful. Vietnam's coastline is a source of national pride and holds immense potential for socio-economic development. We must join hands to protect and sustainably develop Vietnam's coastal tourism, ensuring that Vietnam's coastline remains an alluring destination for both domestic and international visitors.
