The forest area map of Vietnam

  • The forest

    - The remaining primeval forests in Vietnam serve as the habitat for many endangered species of flora and fauna, from Asian elephants to species facing high risks of extinction like the Saola.
    - Every year, new species are still being discovered in the forests of Vietnam, such as the Elfin mountain toad and the crocodile lizard. However, according to Global Forest Watch (GFW), from 2001 to 2018, Vietnam lost over 2.6 million hectares of forest, equivalent to a 16% decrease in forest area compared to 2000.

    • The forest

  • The Ocean

    - With a coastline stretching over 3,000 kilometers and an exclusive economic zone extending over 12 nautical miles, Vietnam is situated in a biologically diverse hotspot of tropical marine regions, possessing abundant fishery resources and diverse marine ecosystems such as mangroves, coral reefs, and seagrass beds. However, despite Vietnam only tapping into a portion of its economic potential from the sea, severe resource depletion and ocean pollution are currently occurring.

    - They are currently implementing the Fisheries Improvement Project (FIP), collaborating with businesses and the government of Vietnam to address issues such as overfishing and ensure the sustainability of the yellowfin tuna species. The FIP conducts assessments of fish stocks, levels of untargeted catch, threats to ecosystems, etc.; advocates for better compliance with regulations and law enforcement; and implements co-management models with local fishing communities.

    • The ocean

  • The wildlife

    - Vietnam is home to a wide variety of wildlife species. Every year, new species are discovered and documented.

    - However, this precious biodiversity is under threat due to habitat loss, deforestation, and forest degradation, as well as widespread illegal hunting. Once abundant, the population of Asian elephants in Vietnam has rapidly declined to just over a hundred individuals outside of the wild.
